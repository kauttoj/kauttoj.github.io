About me

I'm a data scientist and AI specialist interested in solving problems using computational and statistical methods. With a multidisciplinary background in physics, mathematics, computer science, and neuroscience, I integrate advanced AI, quantitative analysis and data science methodologies to drive innovation and deliver actionable insights.

Currently, I work as a Senior Researcher at Haaga-Helia University of Applied Sciences in the Digital Transition & AI research area. I received my PhD in Statistical Physics from the University of Jyväskylä in 2012 and worked as a postdoctoral researcher at Aalto University and Carnegie Mellon University. My research spans neuroscience, cognitive sciences, psychology, and applied artificial intelligence.

I'm involved in multiple RDI projects where I combine research, developent, advisory/consultation, education and quantitive analysis.

I'm always eager to learn more to expand my knowledge and master new skills. Let's connect if you're interested in collaborating on research, development, and innovation projects!

Research Projects

  • Recent projects (2020s)

  • GAIK – Generative AI for Knowledge Management

    2025 — 2027 | ERDF-funded GenAI toolkit for SME knowledge management

    ERDF-funded project developing a Generative AI toolkit to solve critical business challenges in knowledge management. Creates intelligent services for internal processes and customer experience improvement, tailored for Finnish SMEs.

    Role: Researcher, developing AI agents for knowledge management

  • Kone Johtajana / RoboBoss

    2025 — 2026 | Algorithmic management and AI in leadership

    Research on algorithmic management and AI in leadership, particularly in knowledge work. Large-scale study examining benefits and risks when decision-making shifts from human managers to AI systems.

    Role: Researcher, collecting data with statistical modeling

  • UPBEAT

    2024 — 2026 | AI-powered entrepreneurship training (Finland & Estonia)

    AI-powered entrepreneurship training for immigrants in Finland and Estonia. Uses AI both as a learning tool and to teach entrepreneurship skills like market analysis and business planning.

    Role: Researcher, developing tailored learning plans with AI

  • AI-Assisted News Content Targeting

    2024 — 2025 | Generative AI for journalism accessibility

    Research on using generative AI to target news content to different consumer groups, especially immigrants and youth. Focuses on plain language news and improving accessibility of journalism to strengthen democracy.

    Role: Leader and researcher, developing AI pipelines and collecting data with statistical modeling

  • FAIR – Finnish AI Region

    2022 — 2025 | European Digital Innovation Hub (EDIH)

    European Digital Innovation Hub providing free/subsidized services to SMEs for digital and green transformation. Focuses on AI, cybersecurity, HPC, and XR technologies, covering all of Finland with emphasis on Southern Finland.

    Role: Researcher and consultant for SMEs

  • AI-TIE — Supporting SMEs with an AI Innovation Ecosystem

    2021 — 2023 | Clean Industry & Health/Social Care focus

    Supporting SMEs in leveraging AI for business development through an AI innovation ecosystem. Project focused on clean industry and health/social care sectors, providing training, mentoring, and acceleration programs.

    Role: Researcher and consultant for SMEs

  • AI-Forum — AI’s role in digital services and workplace transformation

    2021 — 2023 | Multi-university collaboration network

    Multi-university collaboration network researching AI's role in digital services and workplace transformation. Organized seminars, webinars, publications, and pilot projects to produce new competencies for work life.

    Role: Co-leader and researcher

  • Earlier Projects (2010s)

  • MICrONS Brain Reverse-Engineering

    2016 — 2021 | $12M IARPA-funded (Carnegie Mellon)

    $12M IARPA-funded Carnegie Mellon project to reverse-engineer neural circuits by recording tens of thousands of neurons in mice visual cortex. Aimed to improve machine learning by understanding how brains learn efficiently.

    2009 — 2014 | Aalto University interdisciplinary brain research

    Interdisciplinary brain research combining neuroimaging, filmmaking, and economics. Used cinema as naturalistic stimulus to study human cognitive processes, social interaction, and decision-making.
